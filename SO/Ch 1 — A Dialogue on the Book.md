# Ch 1 — A Dialogue on the Book

> [!abstract] One-sentence summary
> A playful Socratic dialogue that sets up the book's organizing metaphor: **three easy pieces** — [[Virtualization]], [[Concurrency]], [[Persistence]] — a nod to Feynman's physics lectures ("Six Easy Pieces").

## What the Chapter Introduces

- The **three pieces** as the unified framework for the whole book:
  - [[Virtualization]] — how the OS turns physical resources into nicer virtual ones.
  - [[Concurrency]] — how to correctly juggle many simultaneous actors.
  - [[Persistence]] — how to save data so it survives crashes and time.
- The book's pedagogical stance: **read before class, re-read later, and practice with projects**. Ideas stick by *putting them into action*, not by passive reading.
- The **dialogue format** itself as a didactic device — used throughout the book to give a different voice to review material. See [[OSTEP Book Conventions]].

## Crux of the Problem

The chapter doesn't pose a technical crux (it's a dialogue). But it frames the book's **meta-crux**: *what does an OS do, and how?* — answered across the three pieces.
